Neural-IMLS: Learning Implicit Moving Least-Squares for Surface Reconstruction from Unoriented Point clouds

Zixiong Wang1 , Pengfei Wang1 , Qiujie Dong1 , Junjie Gao1 , Shuangmin Chen2 , Shiqing Xin1 , Changhe Tu1 Wenping Wang3
1Shandong University
2Qingdao University of Science and Technology
3Texas A&M University
ArXiv
arXiv Code PDF


We present Neural-IMLS, a novel approach that learns reliable SDF from point clouds directly in a self-supervised fashion. Unlike other learning-based methods (Points2Surf, Neural-Pull, and DeepMLS), it has the ability to reconstruct smooth and watertight surfaces with noisy input.

Abstract

Surface reconstruction from noisy, non-uniformly, and unoriented point clouds is a fascinating yet difficult problem in computer vision and computer graphics. In this paper, we propose Neural-IMLS, a novel approach that learning noiseresistant signed distance function (SDF) for reconstruction. Instead of explicitly learning priors with the ground-truth signed distance values, our method learns the SDF from raw point clouds directly in a self-supervised fashion by minimizing the loss between the couple of SDFs, one obtained by the implicit moving least-square function (IMLS) and the other by our network. Finally, a watertight and smooth 2-manifold triangle mesh is yielded by running Marching Cubes.We conduct extensive experiments on various benchmarks to demonstrate the performance of Neural-IMLS, especially for point clouds with noise.

Pipeline

The self-supervised pipeline of our method. For a query point q (red), we search its nearest neighbors p_{i} (yellow) who are inside the ball (green) centered at query point q with radius r from input point clouds. The supervision signal is provided from the IMLS function g(q) using the gradients (\nabla f(q)), (\nabla f(p_{i})) computed by back-propagation and the neural network is updated by minimizing the error between f(q) and g(q).

Applications

Qualitative results of 3D point clouds reconstruction on ABC var-noise dataset.
